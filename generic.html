Bucket Hat

Materials:
Worsted weight yarn (amount depends on hat size)
Crochet hook (size appropriate for yarn weight)
Yarn needle
Stitch marker (optional)
Stitches used:
Single crochet (sc)
Increase (inc) - typically two single crochets in one stitch
Magic circle (optional)
Instructions:
Crown:
Make a magic circle or chain 4 and slip stitch to join (for a more defined center).
Round 1: Work 8 single crochets (sc) into the magic circle or first chain (8 sc).
Round 2: Increase (inc) in each stitch around (16 sc).
Rounds 3-10:
Increase every other stitch around. The number of increases per round will vary depending on your desired hat size. Here's a common pattern:
Round 3: Increase in every other stitch (24 sc)
Round 4: Increase in every third stitch (32 sc)
Round 5: Increase in every fourth stitch (40 sc)
Continue increasing every nth stitch as needed until you reach your desired crown width. A good way to measure is to try the hat on your head and see if it fits comfortably.
Body:
Round 11: Once you've reached your desired crown width, work one sc in each stitch around without increasing (stitch count remains the same).
Rounds 12- (x): Continue working rounds of sc without increasing until the hat reaches the depth you desire. Try the hat on as you go to gauge depth.
Brim:
Round (x+1): Here, you'll switch to working in the back loops only (BLO) for a nice brim curve. Crochet one sc in each BLO around. Rounds (x+2) and up: Switch back to working through both loops (sc) and continue for a few rounds to establish the brim width. You can adjust the number of rounds to make the brim wider or narrower.
Finishing:
Fasten off and weave in ends.
You can add a rolled brim by folding the brim edge inwards and crocheting a round of sc through both loops to secure it.
Tips:
Use a stitch marker to mark the beginning of each round, especially for the brim section.
You can adjust the number of increases and rounds to customize the hat size and brim width.
There are many video tutorials available online that can visually guide you through the process.
This is a basic pattern, and you can find many variations online. For example, some patterns use double crochet instead of single crochet, or incorporate a contrasting color change for the brim.
Happy crocheting!
