Beanie

Materials:
Worsted weight yarn (around 100 yards)
Crochet hook (size recommended for your yarn weight)
Yarn needle (for weaving in ends)
Optional: tape measure, stitch markers (helpful for keeping track of rounds)
Stitches used:
Chain (ch)
Single crochet (sc)
Slip stitch (sl st)
Size:
This pattern fits an adult head with a circumference of approximately 20-22 inches. To adjust the size, make more or fewer starting chains for the brim.
Instructions:
Start with the brim:
Chain 40 (or adjust for head size).
Sl st into the first chain to form a ring.
Create the brim:
Work sc evenly around the ring, sl st to join at the end (should have 40 sc).
Ch 1 (does not count as a stitch), sc in each sc around, sl st to join.
Repeat row 2 for 2-3 more rows, or until the brim reaches desired width.
Body of the beanie:
Continue working sc in each sc around, but do not join at the end of the row. Instead, turn your work and crochet up the side of the beanie.
Repeat sc in each sc around for many rows, until the beanie reaches the desired height (typically stopping a few inches from the top). Tip: Try the beanie on as you go to get the perfect fit.
Shape the crown:
We will decrease stitches to create the rounded crown. There are a couple of ways to do this. Here’s a simple method:
Row 1: Sc in the first two stitches, sc2tog (single crochet two together) – repeat sc2tog around until the end of the row (decreases by half the number of stitches).
Row 2: Sc in each stitch around.
Row 3: Repeat Row 1 (sc2tog).
Continue alternating rows 2 and 3 until you have only a few stitches remaining.
Close the top:
Cut yarn, leaving a long tail. Thread the tail onto your yarn needle.
Sew through the remaining stitches at the top of the beanie, pulling gently to close. Weave in all remaining ends.
Finishing touches:
You can add a pom-pom or other embellishment to the top of your beanie for a fun touch.
To fold the brim, fold up the desired amount of brim and sew a few stitches in place to keep it folded.
Tips:
Be sure to maintain a consistent stitch tension throughout your project.
Use a stitch marker to mark the beginning of each round to help you keep track of your place.
You can easily customize this pattern by using different colors of yarn or adding stripes.
There are many resources available online with video tutorials that can help you visualize each step of the crocheting process. Search for "[crochet beanie tutorial]" to find helpful videos.
Happy crocheting!
